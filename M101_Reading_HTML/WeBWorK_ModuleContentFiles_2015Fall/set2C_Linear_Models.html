Linear Models

Now that you have an understanding of lines, we will consider linear models. A linear model is an equation for a line that is connected to a real-world situation or data. What does this mean?

Recall when we write the equation for a line:

$$y=mx+b$$

that $y$ represents the dependent variable (sometimes called the "response" variable) and $x$ represents the independent variable (sometimes called the "predictor"). Plugging in a particular value of $x$ gives, or "predicts," a corresponding value for the response $y$. The value of the slope $m$ of the line represents the estimated change in the response variable $y$ for a one-unit change in the predictor variable $x$.

Consider some examples.

Example 2C-1: Suppose you are driving from Caldwell to LaGrande, OR to watch the Yotes play a game against Eastern Oregon University. When you reach Ontario, OR (which is 30 miles into the drive) you are able to set your cruise control at 65 miles per hour (mph) for the remainder of the trip. The linear function $D=65t + 30$ represents your distance from Caldwell $t$ hours after turning on the cruise control. Notice when $t=0$, your distance $D=30$ which corresponds to you being 30 miles from Caldwell (and in Ontario).

Find the slope for the model. Recognize the model as a line in slope-intercept form, $y=mx+b$.
$D=65t+30$ The response is $D$ your distance from Caldwell, the predictor is $t$ the time you have had on the cruise
$m=65$ Our Solution

Example 2C-2: Continue with the linear model $D=65t + 30$ representing your distance $D$ (in miles) from Caldwell $t$ hours after cruise. Describe what the slope means.

$m=65$ mph is positive Recall slope is $\dfrac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}}=\frac{\text{change in } D\text{ miles}}{\text{change in } t\text{ hour}}$
Your distance increases at a rate of $65$ miles for each hour in cruise Our Solution

Example 2C-3: Continue with the linear model $D=65t + 30$ representing your distance $D$ (in miles) from Caldwell $t$ hours after using cruise. How many hours after setting your cruise control will you be $160$ miles from Caldwell?

$D=$ distance from Caldwell Recall what the response variable represents
When is $160=D$? We are asked to determine the time for a particular distance
$160=65t+30$ Solve for $t$: subtract $30$ from both sides
$130=65t$ Divide both sides by $65$
$2=t$ State what this means in context
After $t=2$ hours of cruise control you are $160$ miles from Caldwell Our solution

Example 2C-4: A study of king penguins looked for a relationship between how deep the penguins dive to seek food and how long they stay underwater. The data from the study produced the linear model $DD = 0.0072D + 3$, where $DD$ represents the dive duration in minutes (min) and $D$ represents the depth in meters (m).

Find the slope for the model. Recognize the model as a line in slope-intercept form, $y=mx+b$.
$DD = 0.0072D + 3$ The response is $DD$ the dive duration (min), the predictor is $D$ the depth (m)
$m=0.0072$ Our Solution

Example 2C-5: Continue with the linear model $$DD = 0.0072D + 3$, where $DD$ represents the dive duration in minutes (min) and $D$ represents the depth in meters (m). Describe what the slope means.

$m=0.0072$ min/m is positive Recall slope is $\dfrac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}}=\frac{\text{change in } DD \text{ min}}{\text{change in } D\text{ m}}$
The dive duration increases at a rate of $0.0072$ minute for each additional meter in depth Our Solution

Example 2C-6: Continue with the linear model $$DD = 0.0072D + 3$, where $DD$ represents the dive duration in minutes (min) and $D$ represents the depth in meters (m). According to the model, how long does a typical dive to a depth of $300$ meters last?

$DD=$ dive duration Recall what the response variable represents
$D=$ depth Recall what the predictor variable represents
$D=300$ m We are given a depth and asked to determine the duration for that depth
$DD=0.0072(300) + 3$ Plug the given depth into the model and simplify
$DD=5.16$ State what this means in context
The dive duration is predicted to be $5.16$ minutes for a depth of $300$ meters Our solution

Often researchers record observations over time and use a linear model to represent the situation. Consider some examples.

Example 2C-7: In 1980, the gross domestic product (GDP) of the United States was 3 trillion dollars. In 1990 it was 6 trillion dollars. Let the $x$-axis denote time and the $y$-axis the GDP (in trillions). Let $x=0$ correspond to the year 1980. Identify points that represent the data.

$x=0$, $y=3$ Corresponds to the year 1980 with a GDP of 3 trillion
$(0,3)$ Points in the plane are written $(x,y)$
$x=10$, $y=6$ Corresponds to the year 1990 (ten years after 1980) with a GDP of 6 trillion
$(10,6)$ Points in the plane are written $(x,y)$
$(0,3)$ and $(10,6)$ Our Solution

Example 2C-8: Continue with the GDP example. Find the linear equation determined by the two points $(0,3)$ and $(10,6)$ we found.

First find the slope between $(0, 3)$ and $(10,6)$ Identify $x_1, y_1, x_2, y_2$
$(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$ Use slope formula $m=\dfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$
$m=\dfrac{6-3}{10-0}$ Simplify
$m=\dfrac{3}{10}=.3$ Now find the $y$-intercept
$(0,3)$ The first point is the value of $y$ when $x=0$ which means $b=3$, the $y$-intercept
$y=0.3x+3$ Our solution

Example 2C-9: Continue with the GDP example. Use the linear equation we found to determine the year when the GDP will reach $13.5$ trillion dollars.

$y=$ GDP (in trillions) Recall what the response variable represents
$x=$ time (in years after 1980) Recall what the predictor variable represents
When will $13.5=y$? We are asked to determine the time for a particular GDP
$13.5=.3x+3$ Solve for $x$: subtract $3$ from both sides
$10.5=.3x$ Divide both sides by .3
$35=x$ $x$ is years after 1980
$1980+ 35 = 2015$ State what this means in context
Using the linear model, the GDP will be 13.5 trillion in the year 2015 Our solution

Example 2C-10: An advertiser goes to a printer and is charged $\$47$ for $100$ copies of one flyer and $\$63$ for $259$ copies of another flyer. Let the $x$-axis denote copies and the $y$-axis the cost. Identify points that represent the data.

$x=100$, $y=47$ Corresponds to the cost $\$47$ for $100$ copies
$(100,47)$ Points in the plane are written $(x,y)$
$x=259$, $y=63$ Corresponds to the cost $\$63$ for $259$ copies
$(259,63)$ Points in the plane are written $(x,y)$
$(100,47)$ and $(259,63)$ Our Solution

Example 2C-11: Continue with the copies example. Find the linear equation determined by the two points $(100,47)$ and $(259,63)$ we found.

First find the slope between $(100, 47)$ and $(259,63)$ Identify $x_1, y_1, x_2, y_2$
$(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$ Use slope formula $m=\dfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$
$m=\dfrac{63-47}{259-100}$ Simplify
$m=\dfrac{16}{159}\approx .1$ Now find the $y$-intercept, $b$
$y=0.1x+b$ Choose one of the points to plug into the line
$(100,47)$ $(x,y)$, plug the corresponding values in for $x$ and $y$
$47=0.1(100)+b$ Simplify
$47=10+b$ Subtract $10$ from both sides
$37=b$ Write the equation with the derived $m$ and $b$
$y=0.1x+37$ Our solution
