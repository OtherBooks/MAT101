Linear Equations

Applications are better known as "story problems." Story problems can be challenging, but are a powerful use of mathematics. Often it takes a bit of practice to convert the English sentence into a mathematical sentence. This is what we will focus on here with some basic number and parts problems.

A few important phrases are described below that can give us clues for how to set up a problem.

  • A number (or unknown, a value, etc) often becomes our variable
  • Is (or other forms of is: was, will be, are, etc) often represents equals ($=$)

    • $x$ is $5$ becomes $x=5$

  • More than often represents addition and is usually built backwards, writing the second part plus the first

    • Three more than a number becomes $x+3$

  • Less than often represents subtraction and is usually built backwards as well, writing the second part minus the first

    • Four less than a number becomes $x-4$

Using these key phrases we can take a number problem and set up an equation and solve.

Example 1C-1:

If $28$ less than five times a certain number is $232$, what is the number?

$5x-28$ Subtraction is built backwards, multiply the unknown by $5$
$5x-28=232$ Is translates to equals
$\,\,\,\,\,\underline{+28\,\,+28}$ Add 28 to both sides
$5x=260$ The variable is multiplied by $5$
$\overline{5}\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\overline{5}\,\,\,$ Divide both sides by $5$
$x=52$ The number is $52$.

This same idea can be extended to a more involved problem as shown in the next example.

Example 1C-2:

Fifteen more than three times a number is the same as ten less than six times the number. What is the number?

$3x+15$ First, addition is built backwards
$6x-10$ Then, subtraction is also built backwards
$3x+15=6x-10$ Is between the parts tells us they must be equal
$\underline{-3x \,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,-3x}\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,$ Subtract smaller $3x$ from both sides so variable is on one side
$15=3x-10$ Now we have a two-step equation
$\underline{+10\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\, +10}$ Add $10$ to both sides
$25=3x$ The variable is multiplied by $3$
$\overline{3}\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\overline{ 3}$ Divide both sides by $3$
$\frac{25}{3}=x$ Our number is $\frac{25}{3}$



Practical Percent Problems

There are three basic types of percent problems:
1. Find a given percent of a given number. For example, find $25\%$ of $640$.
2. Find a percent given two numbers. For example, $15$ is what percent of $50$?
3. Find a number that is a given percent of another number. For example, $10\%$ of what number is $12$?

Let’s review the first of these types (these were considered in 0C Percents and Estimation): 1. Find a given percent of a given number.

Example 1C-3 (and 0C-5):
What number is $25\%$ of $640$? Translate the words into an equation: $x$ represents what number, $=$ represents is, $\cdot$ represents of
$x=25\%\cdot 640$ Change $25\%$ to a decimal
$x=.25\cdot 640$ Multiply
$x=160$ Our Solution


Example 1C-4 (and 0C-6):
Find $175\%$ of $30$. Translate the words into an equation: $x$ represents the number to find and $\cdot$ represents of
$x=175\%\cdot 30$ Change $175\%$ to a decimal
$x=1.75\cdot 30$ Multiply
$x=52.5$ Our Solution


Now we’ll address our second item on the list: 2. Find a percent given two numbers.

Example 1C-5:
$15$ is what percent of $50$? Translate the words into an equation: $=$ represents is, $x$ represents what percent, and $\cdot$ represents of
$15=x\cdot 50$ solve our equation for $x$: Divide both sides by $50$
$\dfrac{15}{50}=\dfrac{x\cdot 50}{50}$ Simplify the right-hand side: $\dfrac{50}{50}=1$
$\dfrac{15}{50}=x$ Divide $\dfrac{15}{50}=0.3$
$0.3 = x$ Change $0.3$ to a percent.
$30\% = x$ Our Solution


Example 1C-06:
What percent of $80$ is 10? Translate the words into an equation: $x$ represents what percent, $\cdot$ represents of, and $=$ represents is
$x\cdot 80=10$ solve our equation for $x$: Divide both sides by $80$
$\dfrac{x\cdot 80}{80}=\dfrac{10}{80}$ Simplify the left-hand side: $\dfrac{80}{80}=1$
$x=\dfrac{10}{80}$ Divide $\dfrac{10}{80}=0.125$
$x=0.125$ Change $0.125$ to a percent.
$x=12.5\%$ Our Solution


Now we’ll consider the third item on the list: 3. Find a number that is a given percent of another number.

Example 1C-7:
$10\%$ of what number is $12$? Translate the words into an equation: $\cdot$ represents of, $x$ represents what number, and $=$ represents is
$10\% \cdot x=12$ Change $10\%$ to a decimal
$.10 \cdot x=12$ solve our equation for $x$: Divide both sides by 0.1
$\dfrac{.1}{.1}\cdot x=\dfrac{12}{.1}$ Simplify both sides of the equation: $\dfrac{.1}{.1}=1$ and $\dfrac{12}{.1}=120$
$x=120$ Our Solution


Example 1C-8:
$37.5\%$ of what number is $57$? Translate the words into an equation: $\cdot$ represents of, $x$ represents what number, and $=$ represents is
$37.5\% \cdot x=57$ Change $37.5\%$ to a decimal
$.375 \cdot x=57$ solve our equation for $x$: Divide both sides by 0.375
$\dfrac{.375}{.375}\cdot x=\dfrac{57}{.375}$ Simplify both sides of the equation: $\dfrac{.375}{.375}=1$ and $\dfrac{57}{.375}=152$
$x=152$ Our Solution


Example 1C-9: $35$ millilitres of a $60$ millilitre solution is hydrochloric acid. What percent of the solution is hydrochloric acid?
$\color{green}{\text{Amount of hydrochloric acid }} \color{red}{\text{is }}$
$\color{blue}{\text{what percent }} \color{red}{\text{of }} \color{green}{\text{the total amount of the solution}}$ 		Translate the problem into symbols:
$p$ represents the percent, $=$ represents is, and $\cdot$ represents of,
$\color{green}{35} \color{red}{=} \color{blue}{p} \color{red}{\cdot} \color{green}{60}$ Solve for $p$: Divide both sides by $60$
$\overline{60}\phantom{12}\overline{60}$ Convert the fraction to a decimal
$p=\dfrac{35}{60}\approx .583333$ Convert the decimal to a percentage
$p=58.333\%$ Our Solution

Percent Increase or Decrease

A person’s salary can increase by a percentage. A town’s population can decrease by a percentage. A clothing firm can discount its apparel. Consider the following examples.

Example 1C-10: A salesperson is granted a 5% salary increase. If the salesperson’s current salary is $4,000 per month, what will be his new salary?
Salary increase is $5\%$ of original salary Translate the problem into symbols: $x$ represents the Salary increase, $=$ represents is, and $\cdot$ represents of,
$x=5\%\cdot 4000$ Change $5\%$ to a decimal
$x=.05\cdot 4000$ Multiply: $0.05 \cdot 4000 = 200$
$x=200$ Recall: $x$ represents the Salary increase
New salary = $4000+200$ Compute the New salary using the current salary and the Salary increase = $x$
$\$4200$ Our Solution


Example 1C-11: Due to a mill closure, the population of Silvertown decreases by $8.5\%$. If the original population was $10,200$ hardy souls, what is the new population?
Population decrease is $8.5\%$ of original population Translate the problem into symbols: $x$ represents the Population decrease, $=$ represents is, and $\cdot$ represents of,
$x=8.5\%\cdot 10200$ Change $8.5\%$ to a decimal
$x=.085\cdot 10200$ Multiply: $0.85\cdot 10200 = 867$
$x=867$ Recall: $x$ represents the Population decrease
New population = $10200-867$ Compute the New population using the original population and the Population decrease = $x$
$9333$ Our Solution


Discount

Another important application of percent is the discounting of goods.

Example 1C-12: A pair of skis is marked at $\$310$. However, a sign in the shop indicates that skis are being discounted at $15\%$. What will be the new selling price of the skis?
Discount is $15\%$ of original marked price Translate the problem into words and symbols: $D$ represents the Discount, $=$ represents is, and $\cdot$ represents of,
$D=15\%\cdot 310$ Change $15\%$ to a decimal
$D=.15\cdot 310$ Multiply: $0.15\cdot 310 = 46.50$
$D=46.50$ Recall: $D$ represents the Discount
New selling price = $310-46.50$ Compute the New selling price using the original selling price and the Discount = $D$
$\$263.50$ Our Solution


Absolute Value Applications

Recall from reading 1B absolute value is defined as distance from zero. We now consider some absolute value equations and inequalities.

Example 1C-13: Suppose the distance between $x$ and $4$ is exactly $7$. Find $x$.
$\color{red}{\text{distance }} \color{green}{\text{between }} x \text{ and } 4 \color{red}{\text{ is exactly }} \color{blue}{7}$ Translate the problem into symbols: distance is absolute value, between is subtraction, $=$ represents is
$\color{red}{|} x \color{green}{-} 4\color{red}{|} \color{red}{=} \color{blue}{7}$ Absolute value can be positive or negative
$x-4=7$ or $x-4=-7$ Two equations to solve
$x-4=7$ $\,\,\,\,\,$ $x-4=-7$
$\underline{+4\,+4}$ $\,\,\,\,\,$ $\underline{+4\,\,\,\,\,+4}$
$x=11$ $\,\,\,\,$ or $x=-3$

Thus, we have two $x$-values that work: $x = 11,-3$

Example 1C-14: Suppose the distance between $x$ and zero is less than or equal to $6$. Find $x$.
$\color{red}{\text{distance }} \color{green}{\text{between }} x \text{ and } 0 \color{red}{\text{ is less than or equal to }} \color{blue}{6}$ Translate the problem into symbols: distance is absolute value, between is subtraction, $\leq$ represents less than or equal to
$\color{red}{|} x \color{green}{-} 0\color{red}{|} \color{red}{\leq} \color{blue}{6}$ Absolute value is less, use three part
$|x|\leq 6$ Simplify the absolute value expression
$-6\leq x \leq 6$ Inequality notation

Thus, we have an interval of $x$-values that work: $-6\leq x \leq 6$.

Example 1C-15: Suppose the distance between $x$ and $-14$ is greater than $3$. Find $x$.
$\color{red}{\text{distance }} \color{green}{\text{between }} x \text{ and } $-14$ \color{red}{\text{ is greater than }} \color{blue}{3}$ Translate the problem into symbols: distance is absolute value, between is subtraction, $\gt$ represents greater than
$\color{red}{|} x \color{green}{-} (-14)\color{red}{|} \color{red}{\gt} \color{blue}{3}$ Simplify $-(-14)=+14$, absolute value is greater, use OR
$x+14 \gt 3$ OR $x+14\lt -3$ Subtract $14$
$\underline{-14\,-14\phantom{123}-14\phantom{1}-14}$ $\,$
$x\gt -11$ OR $x\lt -17$ Compound inequality
$(-\infty,-17)\cup(-11,\infty)$ Interval notation

Thus, we have a compound interval of $x$-values that work: $(-\infty,-17)\cup(-11,\infty)$.
